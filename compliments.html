How to Accept a Compliment Without Feeling Awkward

Let’s be honest—accepting a compliment can sometimes feel more uncomfortable than giving one. Maybe you don’t want to sound arrogant, or you simply don’t know what to say. But here’s the truth: compliments are social gifts. When you downplay or reject them, you’re not only dismissing yourself—you’re also turning away genuine appreciation.

This guide breaks down the key lessons from Science of People’s article on how to accept compliments gracefully, confidently, and authentically.

Why We Struggle to Accept Compliments

Many of us instinctively brush off compliments with phrases like “Oh, it was nothing!” or “I just got lucky.” We do this to appear humble or because we doubt our own worth. But rejecting a compliment can make the other person feel awkward, and it blocks an opportunity for connection and confidence.

The goal isn’t to brag—it’s to accept kindness with gratitude.

How to Give Better Compliments (That Are Easier to Receive)

Before learning how to accept compliments, it helps to understand how to give them well. Great compliments feel specific, sincere, and low-pressure. Here are four quick rules:

  • Be specific

    Go beyond “Good job.” Say what, exactly, you admired.

  • Acknowledge effort, not just talent.

    Compliments like “You worked really hard on this” feel more meaningful.

  • Choose the right moment.

    Compliment in private when possible to avoid putting someone on the spot.

  • Expect nothing in return.

    A true compliment doesn’t come with strings attached.

7 Graceful Ways to Accept a Compliment

If you’ve ever felt unsure what to say when someone praises you, these seven simple strategies keep things natural and genuine:

  1. Just say “Thank you.” A sincere response is often enough.
  2. Add a small detail to show thoughtfulness or engagement.
  3. Share the credit if others contributed to your success.
  4. Express how the compliment makes you feel emotionally.
  5. Return a genuine compliment after receiving one, if it feels natural.
  6. Ask a follow-up question to continue the conversation.
  7. Use open body language—smile, maintain eye contact, and stay relaxed.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Here are some habits that can make accepting compliments harder than it needs to be:

  1. Deflecting praise with comments like “It was nothing.”
  2. Joking or downplaying your achievement.
  3. Turning the moment into a competition or comparison.
  4. Over-explaining instead of simply saying thanks.

The Secret to Accepting Compliments Gracefully

Think of compliments as mini moments of connection. Each one is a chance to strengthen relationships and reinforce your self-worth. Start by accepting every compliment with a warm “thank you,” then add a bit of detail or emotion when it feels right. Over time, this practice becomes natural and empowering.

Final Thoughts

Accepting compliments isn’t about ego—it’s about gratitude. When you receive praise with warmth and humility, you honor both yourself and the person who offered it. Practice saying “thank you” with confidence—it might just change how you see yourself and how others see you.
